Berberine is extracted from plants and is believed to reduce LDL (Low-density lipoprotein) cholesterol and triglycerides, however, you must consider the potential side effects and interactions if you are on medications.

Fish oil supplements and omega-3 fatty acids are effective for slowing down the rate of triglyceride production in the liver and decreasing plaque formation in the arteries. The supplement source of omega-3 fatty acids is generally fatty fish, flaxseed, and walnuts. 

Flaxseed, ground

Ground flaxseed is rich in omega-3 fatty acids and fiber, which can help reduce LDL cholesterol levels. However, ensure that you consume ground flaxseed or flaxseed oil for optimal digestion, otherwise, it can be difficult to digest it.

Green tea or green tea extract

Green tea supplements have active compounds called catechins and flavonoids that can potentially reduce the LDL cholesterol levels. While it is also important to note down that it has caffeine and should be avoided if you are sensitive to caffeine. 

Fiber Supplements

There are various fiber supplements like psyllium, methylcellulose, and wheat dextrin, that can contribute to lowering overall cholesterol levels and LDL cholesterol.

Garlic Supplements

Garlic is a natural supplement to lower cholesterol. However, if you are on blood-thinning medications or preparing for surgery, you must consult healthcare experts before starting the garlic supplements.
